Key information

This document provides information about the data underlying the Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT) for West Midlands. The data were generated on 2016-12-02 and this document was created on 2017-03-20. The PCT is an open source tool for sustainable transport planning, released under the conditions of the Affero GPL. Both the pct and pct-shiny can be modified by others as long as attribution is made to the original.

This version of the PCT uses origin-destination (OD) data on travel to work from the 2011 Census. The dataset reports the number of people travelling by different modes from Middle Super Output Area zones (MSOA, average size 3325 commuters). There were 929,974 commuters living in West Midlands recorded in the 2011 Census. All of these are represented in the zones data produced by the PCT.

In West Midlands there are 3224 flows between MSOAs that a) start and end in West Midlands, b) have a straight-line (Euclidean) distance of less than 20km and a fast-route distance less than 30km, and c) contain more than 50 commuters (by any mode, counting commuters in both directions). These 3224 between-zone MSOA flows are visualised as Straight Lines, Routes (fast and quiet) and the Route Network (MSOA) on the interactive map, and account for 39% of all commuters living in West Midlands.

Between-zone MSOA flows exclude within-MSOA travel, when the MSOA zone of origin is the same as the zone of destination. Within-MSOA travel is represented by red points on the map when the lines are shown, and accounts for 5% of commuters in West Midlands. The between-zone MSOA flows visualised as lines and routes on the map also exclude commuters travelling outside West Midlands and people with no fixed place of work.

An Route Network (LSOA) is also available, to complement the MSOA-level lines, routes and route network. This LSOA (for Lower Super Output Area) route network includes all commuters living in West Midlands and travelling <20km, except for those living and working the same LSOA or having no fixed place of work. Unlike the MSOA route network, the LSOA route network includes commuters living in West Midlands but working in another region within England. For more details please see User Manual C.

See Lovelace et al. (2017) for details of the methods used to estimate the cycling, health and carbon impacts of each scenario, and to visualise results at the area, line, route and route network level.

Cycling levels in each scenario for West Midlands

The proportion of people who cycled as their main mode of travel to work baseline (2011 Census) data in West Midlands was 1.7%, compared to the national average of 3.2%. The percentage of cycling expected, based on the distance and hilliness of commutes in the region using the baseline model for England, was 3.1%.

Health and carbon impacts

A modified version of the 2014 HEAT tool was used to calculate health impacts, based on the extra physical activity gained through new cycling trips in each scenario, minus the physical activity displaced in any former walking trips that have now been switched to cycling. CO2 impacts are derived from changes in distance driven per scenario.
