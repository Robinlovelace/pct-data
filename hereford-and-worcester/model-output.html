Key information

This document provides information about the data underlying the Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT) for Hereford and Worcester. The data were generated on 2016-11-11 and this document was created on 2016-11-11. The PCT is an open source tool for sustainable transport planning, released under the conditions of the Affero GPL. Both the pct and pct-shiny can be modified by others as long as attribution is made to the original.

This version of the PCT uses origin-destination (OD) data on travel to work from the 2011 Census. The dataset reports the number of people travelling by different modes from Middle Super Output Area (MSOA) zones. There were 321,525 commuters living in Hereford and Worcester recorded in the 2011 Census. All of these are represented in the zones data produced by the PCT.

In Hereford and Worcester there are 1518 between-zone flows that a) start and end in Hereford and Worcester, b) have a straight-line (Euclidean) distance of less than 20km and a fast-route distance less than 30km, and c) contain more than 10 commuters (by any mode, counting commuters in both directions). These 1518 between-zone flows are visualised as Straight Lines, Routes (fast and quiet) and the Route Network on the interactive map, and account for 49% of all commuters living in Hereford and Worcester.

Between-zone flows exclude within-zone travel, when the zone of origin is the same as the zone of destination. Within-zone travel is represented by red points on the map when the lines are shown, and accounts for 11% of commuters in Hereford and Worcester. The between-zone flows visualised as lines and routes on the map also exclude commuters travelling outside Hereford and Worcester and people with no fixed place of work.

See Lovelace et al. (2016) for details of the methods used to estimate the cycling, health and carbon impacts of each scenario, and to visualise results at the area, line, route and route network level.

Cycling levels in each scenario for Hereford and Worcester

The proportion of people who cycled as their main mode of travel to work baseline (2011 Census) data in Hereford and Worcester was 2.8%, compared to the national average of 3.2%. The percentage of cycling expected, based on the distance and hilliness of commutes in the region using the baseline model for England, was 2.5%.

Health and carbon impacts

A modified version of the 2014 HEAT tool was used to calculate health impacts, based on the extra physical activity gained through new cycling trips in each scenario, minus the physical activity displaced in any former walking trips that have now been switched to cycling. CO2 impacts are derived from changes in distance driven per scenario.
