Key information

This document provides more information about the data underlying the Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT) for Isle-of-wight. The data was generated on 2016-06-03 and this document was created on 2016-06-18. The PCT is an open source tool for sustainable transport planning, released under the conditions of the AGPL Licence.Both the pct and pct-shiny can be modified by others given attribution to the original.

This version of the PCT uses origin-destination (OD) data on travel to work from the 2011 Census. The dataset reports the number of people travelling by different modes from Middle Super Output Area (MSOA) zones. There were 52,278 commuters in the study area recorded in the 2011 Census. All of these are represented in the area data produced by the PCT.

Within Isle-of-wight there are 143 between-zone flows with a straight line distance of less than 30km.

Of these, 139 (97%) are represented as Straight Lines, Routes (fastest and quietest) and the Route Network on the interactive map, capturing 100% of the between-zone commutes < 30 km within Isle-of-wight. The following selection criteria was used to select which flows were visualised:

  • Straight line (Euclidean) distance of less than 20 km.
  • More than 10 commuters (by any mode).

These 139 desire lines account for 61% of all commutes in Isle-of-wight.

Between-zone flows exclude within-zone travel (represented as red points on the map when the lines are shown), which accounts for 18% of commutes in Isle-of-wight, commuters travelling outside Isle-of-wight and people with no fixed place of work.

See (Lovelace et al.) for details of the methods we used to estimate the cycling, health and carbon impacts of each scenario, and to visualise results at the area, line, route and route network level.

The Isle-of-wight region

The average hilliness of the fastest routes in Isle-of-wight 2.72%, compared with a national average of 1.57%.

Cycling levels in each scenario for Isle-of-wight

The proportion of cyclists in the baseline (2011 Census) data in Isle-of-wight is 3.4%, compared to national average of 3.2%. The percentage of cycling expected, based on the distance and hilliness of commutes in the region using the baseline model for England, was 2.1%.

Health and carbon impacts

A modified version of the 2014 HEAT tool was used to calculate health impacts, based on the extra physical activity gained through new cycling trips in each scenario, minus the physical activity displaced in any former walking trips that have now been switched to cycling. CO2 impacts are derived from changes in distance driven per scenario.
