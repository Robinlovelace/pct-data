Key information

This document provides information about the data underlying the Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT) for Cambridgeshire. The data was generated on 2016-06-23 and this document was created on 2016-06-23. The PCT is an open source tool for sustainable transport planning, released under the conditions of the AGPL Licence. Both the pct and pct-shiny can be modified by others given attribution to the original.

This version of the PCT uses origin-destination (OD) data on travel to work from the 2011 Census. The dataset reports the number of people travelling by different modes from Middle Super Output Area (MSOA) zones. There were 351,267 commuters in the study area recorded in the 2011 Census. All of these are represented in the area data produced by the PCT.

Within Cambridgeshire there are 1264 between-zone flows with a straight line distance of less than 30km.

Of these, 1110 (88%) are represented as Straight Lines, Routes (fast and quiet) and the Route Network on the interactive map, capturing 97% of the between-zone commutes < 30 km within Cambridgeshire. Two selection criteria were used to select which were visualised: only flows that had a) a Euclidean (crow-flies) distance of less than 20 km; and b) more than 10 commuters travelling on it (by any mode in both directions). These 1110 flows account for 50% of all commutes in Cambridgeshire.

Between-zone flows exclude within-zone travel, when the zone of origin is the same as the zone of destination. Within-zone travel is represented by red points on the map when the lines are shown, and accounts for 12% of commuters in Cambridgeshire. The between-zone flows visualised lines and routes on the map also exclude commuters travelling outside Cambridgeshire and people with no fixed place of work.

See Lovelace et al. (2016) for details of the methods used to estimate the cycling, health and carbon impacts of each scenario, and to visualise results at the area, line, route and route network level.

Cycling levels in each scenario for Cambridgeshire

The proportion of people who cycled as their main mode of travel to work baseline (2011 Census) data in Cambridgeshire was 9.7%, compared to the national average of 3.2%. The percentage of cycling expected, based on the distance and hilliness of commutes in the region using the baseline model for England, was 4.2%.
